What is the Geological Unit Finder?

The Geological Unit Finder is a program designed to work with a SQL file database containing information about the main geological units of the Neuquén Basin in Argentina. Users can enter various geological properties, and the program will return matching geological units from the dataset. In addition, users can view the properties and bibliographic references for the geological units of interest.

What is the Neuquen Basin?

The Neuquén Basin is a subandean depocenter located in southern South America with almost 7,000 m of Jurassic and Cretaceous sediments. Its depositional history is a cyclical succession of marine incursions alternating with continental episodes. During the Upper Cretaceous, the rise of the Andes Chain started, and the definite closure of the Pacific marine connection ended the history of the basin as a depocenter.

Its history as a receptor basin initiates in the Late Triassic - Early Jurassic. The principal basement lineaments, that influence the inlines of the subsidence, range from the Early-Middle Paleozoic to the Early Permian.

What are its main characteristics?

The Neuquén Basin has a "embayment" underground form, limited by the Andes to the west, by the San Rafael Block to the north and north-east, and by the Somun Cura or North-Patagonian Massif to the south and east.

The principal geological lineament of the basin is the Huincul Dorsal.

The evolution of the Andean Front gives form to the actual morphostructure of the basin, with the thrust and belt front limited to the west by a plio-quaternary depression, and to the east by the Embayment zone. Fault inversion, thrust, and belt generation expose the principal Mesozoic sedimentary sequences.
